Dennis MacAlistair Ritchie

The man who created C programming language

Here's a time line of Dennis's life

  • 1941 September 9 - Dennis MacAlistair Ritchie is born in Bronxville, New York.

  • 1963 - Graduates from Harvard University with a Bachelor of Arts degree in physics and applied mathematics.

  • 1967 - Joins Bell Labs' Computing Sciences Research Center.

  • 1968 - Defends his PhD thesis on "Computational Complexity and Program Structure" at Harvard under the supervision of Patrick C. Fischer.

  • 1970-1972 - Collaborates with Ken Thompson to develop the B programming language.

  • 1973 -Rewrites UNIX in C.

  • 1978 - Co-authors "The C Programming Language" with Brian Kernighan.

  • 1983 - Awarded the ACM Turing Award with Ken Thompson for their creation of the C programming language and Unix operating system.

  • 1999 - Awarded the U.S. National Medal of Technology by President Bill Clinton for their contributions to computing.

  • 2002 -Appointed head of the System Software Research Department at Lucent Technologies.

  • 2007 - Retires from Lucent Technologies.

  • October 12, 2011 - Died in Summit, New Jersey, at the age of 70.
